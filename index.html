Marine Biology: Pacific ocean activity quiz

1. What makes up the Hawaiian Ridge?

A long chain of undersea volcanoes, signifying the presence of meeting tectonic plates.

2. What caused the bend between the the volcanoes of the Emperor Seamounts and the Hawaiian ridge?

The ridge curved when the Pacific Plate shifted and changed direction.

3. What is a hot spot?

A hot spot is a location with high volcanic activity, where hot magma is forced up out of the mantle, cooling to form undersea mounds or islands.

4. Where are hot spots that produce single islands or island groups?

Hot spots associated with ocean ridges create singular islands or island groups because they do not move like plates do, hence not making long chains of islands.
